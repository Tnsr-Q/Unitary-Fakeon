## I. Regularized Complexified Action & Critical Point Deformation

Let the theory be UV-regularized by a hard mode cutoff $\Lambda$, reducing the field space to $N$ complex dimensions $z \in \mathbb{C}^N$ (Fourier coefficients of $h_{\mu\nu}$ in the Barnes-Rivers spin-projector basis). Introduce a formal bookkeeping parameter $\lambda \sim \kappa$ to track perturbative order. The complexified action reads:
$$
S_\lambda(z) = S_0(z) + \lambda V_3(z) + \lambda^2 V_4(z) = \frac{1}{2} z^T M z + \lambda V_3(z) + \lambda^2 V_4(z),
$$
where:
- $M = \text{diag}(\mu_1, \dots, \mu_N)$ is the Hessian of the quadratic action. Physical graviton/scalar modes satisfy $\text{Re}(\mu_j) > 0$; the fakeon spin-2 mode has $\mu_f < 0$ (wrong-sign kinetic term).
- $V_3, V_4$ are homogeneous polynomials of degree 3 and 4 arising from the $C_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}C^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}$ expansion. Their coefficients are bounded by $\Lambda$-dependent norms $\|V_k\| < \infty$.

**Critical Point Deformation:**  
The saddle points satisfy $\nabla S_\lambda(z) = M z + \lambda \nabla V_3(z) + \lambda^2 \nabla V_4(z) = 0$. At $\lambda=0$, $z_0=0$ is the unique perturbative saddle. Since $\det M \neq 0$, the **holomorphic implicit function theorem** guarantees a unique analytic branch $z_\sigma(\lambda)$ for $|\lambda| < \lambda_c$, with expansion:
$$
z_\sigma(\lambda) = -\lambda M^{-1} \nabla V_3(0) - \lambda^2 M^{-1} \left[ \nabla V_4(0) - \frac{1}{2} \nabla^2 V_3(0) M^{-1} \nabla V_3(0) \right] + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^3).
$$
Since $V_3$ is cubic, $\nabla V_3(0)=0$, so $z_\sigma(\lambda) = \mathcal{O}(\lambda^2)$. The perturbative saddle remains exponentially close to the origin.

## II. Perturbative Thimble Deformation Equations

The downward gradient flow defining the Lefschetz thimble $\mathcal{J}_\sigma(\lambda)$ is:
$$
\frac{dz}{d\tau} = -\overline{\nabla S_\lambda(z)}, \quad \tau \in \mathbb{R}_{\geq 0}.
$$
Linearizing around the deformed saddle $z_\sigma(\lambda)$ via $z(\tau) = z_\sigma(\lambda) + \delta z(\tau)$ yields:
$$
\frac{d}{d\tau} \delta z = -\overline{H(\lambda)} \, \delta z + \mathcal{O}(\|\delta z\|^2), \quad H(\lambda) = \nabla^2 S_\lambda(z_\sigma(\lambda)).
$$
The tangent space $T_{z_\sigma}\mathcal{J}_\sigma(\lambda)$ is spanned by eigenvectors of $\overline{H(\lambda)}$ with positive real parts (directions of exponential decay as $\tau \to +\infty$). Let $P_\lambda$ be the spectral projector onto this subspace. By **Kato's perturbation theory**, $P_\lambda$ satisfies the deformation equation:
$$
\frac{dP_\lambda}{d\lambda} = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_\Gamma (z - \overline{H(\lambda)})^{-1} \frac{d\overline{H(\lambda)}}{d\lambda} (z - \overline{H(\lambda)})^{-1} \, dz,
$$
where $\Gamma$ encloses the eigenvalues with $\text{Re}(\mu) > 0$. Explicitly, to $\mathcal{O}(\lambda)$:
$$
P_\lambda = P_0 + \lambda \sum_{j \in \text{phys}} \sum_{k \in \text{fakeon}} \frac{\langle v_j, \overline{\nabla^2 V_3} v_k \rangle}{\overline{\mu_j} - \overline{\mu_k}} |v_j\rangle\langle v_k| + \text{h.c.} + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^2).
$$
The fakeon direction $v_f$ deforms smoothly but remains spectrally isolated from the physical subspace. The thimble $\mathcal{J}_\sigma(\lambda)$ is the image of $\mathcal{J}_\sigma(0)$ under the holomorphic flow generated by this projector deformation.

## III. Morse Index Stability Analysis

The Morse index $\nu(\lambda)$ is the number of eigenvalues of $H(\lambda)$ with negative real part. At $\lambda=0$, $\nu(0)=1$ (the fakeon mode). We prove $\nu(\lambda)=1$ for small $\lambda$.

**Theorem (Index Rigidity):**  
Let $\Delta = \min_{j \neq f} |\mu_f^{(0)} - \mu_j^{(0)}| > 0$ be the spectral gap. If $|\lambda| < \frac{\Delta}{2 \|\nabla^2 V_3\| + 2|\lambda| \|\nabla^2 V_4\|}$, then $\text{Re}(\mu_f(\lambda)) < 0$ and $\text{Re}(\mu_j(\lambda)) > 0$ for all $j \neq f$. Hence $\nu(\lambda) = \nu(0) = 1$.

**Proof:**  
By analytic perturbation theory, eigenvalues satisfy $|\mu_j(\lambda) - \mu_j^{(0)}| \leq |\lambda| \|\nabla^2 V_3\| + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^2)$. The gap condition ensures no eigenvalue crosses the imaginary axis. Since $\mu_f^{(0)} \in \mathbb{R}_{<0}$ and the perturbation is bounded, $\text{Re}(\mu_f(\lambda))$ remains strictly negative. The fakeon direction never rotates into the physical convergence sector. The Morse index is topologically protected perturbatively.

**Consequence for the Fakeon:**  
The negative eigenvalue mandates that convergence along the fakeon direction requires integration along the imaginary axis $z_f = i y_f$ ($y_f \in \mathbb{R}$). This rotation flips the sign of the quadratic form, yielding a convergent Gaussian that evaluates to a real principal value. Index stability guarantees this contour deformation persists order-by-order in $\lambda$.

## IV. Asymptotic Flow Preservation & Convergence Sectors

Thimble convergence at infinity is governed by the highest-degree term in $S_\lambda(z)$. For quadratic gravity, $V_4(z)$ (from $C^4$) dominates:
$$
S_\lambda(z) \sim \lambda^2 V_4(z) \quad \text{as} \quad \|z\| \to \infty.
$$
$V_4(z)$ inherits the sign structure of the kinetic operator: positive-definite on physical modes, negative-definite on the fakeon mode. The downward flow at infinity satisfies:
$$
\frac{dz}{d\tau} \sim -\lambda^2 \overline{\nabla V_4(z)}.
$$
**Lemma (Asymptotic Sector Rigidity):**  
The Stokes wedges (angular sectors in $\mathbb{C}^N$ where $\text{Re}(S_\lambda) \to +\infty$) are determined by the quartic form $V_4$. Since $V_4$ is homogeneous and its signature is fixed by diffeomorphism invariance, the asymptotic convergence directions of $\mathcal{J}_\sigma(\lambda)$ are homotopic to those of $\mathcal{J}_\sigma(0)$ for all $|\lambda| < \lambda_c$.

**Proof Sketch:**  
Write $z = R \hat{z}$ with $\|\hat{z}\|=1$. Then $\text{Re}(S_\lambda) \sim \lambda^2 R^4 \text{Re}(V_4(\hat{z}))$. The set $\{\hat{z} \mid \text{Re}(V_4(\hat{z})) > 0\}$ defines the allowed asymptotic directions. Perturbative $\lambda V_3$ and $S_0$ are $\mathcal{O}(R^3)$ and $\mathcal{O}(R^2)$, respectively, and cannot alter the sign of $\text{Re}(S_\lambda)$ for $R > R_0(\lambda)$. Hence the thimble's ends remain anchored in the same homology class of $H_N(\mathbb{C}^N, \{\text{Re}(S) \gg 0\}; \mathbb{Z})$.

## V. Stokes Phenomena: Conditions for Jumps & Perturbative Isolation

Stokes jumps occur when the integration cycle $\mathcal{C} = \sum_\sigma n_\sigma \mathcal{J}_\sigma$ discontinuously changes coefficients $n_\sigma$. By Picard-Lefschetz theory, this happens only when crossing a **Stokes wall**:
$$
\mathcal{W}_{\sigma\sigma'} = \left\{ \lambda \in \mathbb{C} \;\big|\; \text{Im}\big(S_\lambda(z_\sigma) - S_\lambda(z_{\sigma'})\big) = 0 \;\text{and}\; \exists \text{ flow line connecting } z_\sigma \leftrightarrow z_{\sigma'} \right\}.
$$

**Theorem (Perturbative Stokes Isolation):**  
For $|\lambda| \ll 1$, the perturbative fakeon thimble $\mathcal{J}_0(\lambda)$ does not undergo Stokes jumps. The coefficients $n_\sigma$ remain fixed, and the PV projection is preserved.

**Proof:**  
1. **Action Separation:** At the perturbative saddle, $S_\lambda(z_\sigma) = \mathcal{O}(\lambda^4)$. Any non-perturbative saddle (e.g., complex instantons, Euclidean black holes) has action $S_{\text{np}} \sim \mathcal{O}(1/\lambda^2)$ or $\mathcal{O}(1/G)$. Thus $|\text{Im}(S_\lambda(z_\sigma) - S_\lambda(z_{\text{np}}))| \gg 0$ for small $\lambda$. The imaginary parts are parametrically separated; the Stokes condition $\text{Im}(\Delta S)=0$ cannot be satisfied.
2. **Flow Line Absence:** A connecting trajectory requires a heteroclinic orbit of the gradient flow. Linearization around $z_\sigma$ shows all physical directions decay exponentially, while the fakeon direction grows. No trajectory can reach a distant saddle without crossing a region where $\text{Re}(S)$ increases, violating the downward flow condition.
3. **Fakeon Sector Specificity:** The fakeon eigenvalue $\mu_f(\lambda)$ remains real and negative. No other critical point approaches the origin in the complexified fakeon plane. The relative homology class of $\mathcal{J}_0(\lambda)$ is locally constant.

**Conclusion on Stokes Jumps:**  
Stokes phenomena are non-perturbative effects. In the perturbative regime $|\lambda| < \lambda_c$, the thimble decomposition is analytic in $\lambda$, and the fakeon's purely virtual status (encoded by the imaginary-axis integration and PV evaluation) is topologically stable. Jumps would require $\lambda$ to cross a wall where $\text{Im}(S)$ degenerates, which lies outside the domain of perturbative validity.
